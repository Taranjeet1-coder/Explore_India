Welcome to Ladakh

Ladakh, located in the northernmost region of India, is a place of awe-inspiring landscapes, tranquil monasteries, and rugged mountains. Known as the "Land of High Passes," Ladakh offers a unique blend of adventure and spirituality, making it a must-visit destination for nature lovers and explorers alike.

Transportation to Ladakh

Travel options to Ladakh from LPU:

  • Leh (Ladakh's Capital): ~1,700 km from LPU
    • Flight: ₹7,000 - ₹12,000 (to Leh Kushok Bakula Rimpochee Airport)
    • Bus: ₹1,500 - ₹2,500 (approximately 40-45 hours)
    • Train: There are no direct trains to Ladakh. The nearest railway station is Jammu (approx. 600 km), from where you can take a bus to Leh (approx. 12-14 hours).
  • Magnetic Hill: ~25 km from Leh (approx. 30 minutes by car)
  • Pangong Lake: ~150 km from Leh (approx. 5 hours by car)
  • Nubra Valley: ~120 km from Leh (approx. 4 hours by car)

Top Destinations in Ladakh

Leh Palace

Leh Palace is a historic Tibetan-style palace located in the heart of Leh. The palace offers spectacular views of the Leh town and the surrounding Himalayan peaks. It is a great place to understand Ladakh's rich cultural heritage.

Magnetic Hill

Magnetic Hill is a famous spot where vehicles seem to defy gravity and appear to move uphill on their own. It is one of the most popular natural wonders in Ladakh, drawing travelers and thrill-seekers.

Pangong Lake

Pangong Lake, located at an altitude of 4,350 meters, is one of the most beautiful lakes in the world. Known for its changing colors, the lake stretches across India and China and is a major attraction for tourists in Ladakh.

Nubra Valley

Nubra Valley is a high-altitude desert with stunning landscapes of sand dunes, monasteries, and lush green oases. It is famous for camel rides on Bactrian camels and its picturesque views.
