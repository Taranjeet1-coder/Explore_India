Welcome to Delhi

Delhi, the capital of India, is a bustling metropolis rich in history, culture, and modernity. From historic monuments like the Red Fort and Qutub Minar to bustling markets like Chandni Chowk, Delhi offers a blend of the old and the new.

Transportation to Delhi

Travel options to Delhi from LPU:

  • Delhi: ~300 km from LPU
    • Flight: ₹3,000 - ₹5,000 (to Indira Gandhi International Airport)
    • Bus: ₹800 - ₹1,500 (approximately 6-7 hours)
    • Train: ₹400 - ₹1,500 (to New Delhi Railway Station, around 5-6 hours)

Top Destinations in Delhi

Red Fort

The Red Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most iconic landmarks of India. Built in the 17th century, it symbolizes the Mughal architectural legacy.

Qutub Minar

The Qutub Minar is the tallest brick minaret in the world and a famous symbol of Indo-Islamic Afghan architecture. It is also a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Lotus Temple

The Lotus Temple is a Bahá'í House of Worship known for its lotus flower-like architecture. It is one of the most visited buildings in the world.

Bangla Sahib Gurudwara

Bangla Sahib is one of the most prominent Sikh Gurudwaras in Delhi, known for its serene atmosphere, historical significance, and beautiful architecture.
