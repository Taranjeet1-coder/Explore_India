Welcome to Hawa Mahal

Hawa Mahal, also known as the "Palace of Winds," is a stunning architectural marvel located in Jaipur, Rajasthan. This five-story structure, built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, was designed to allow royal women to observe street festivals and life in the city without being seen. The palace is renowned for its intricately designed lattice windows, which allow cool air to circulate, providing a refreshing breeze even during the hot summers.

Hawa Mahal Details

About Hawa Mahal

The Hawa Mahal, designed by the architect Lal Chand Usta, is made of red and pink sandstone and is one of Jaipur’s most iconic landmarks. The unique feature of the palace is its honeycomb structure, which is punctuated by 953 small windows (jharokhas) adorned with intricate latticework. These windows allow the breeze to flow through the palace, keeping it cool even in the harshest of summers, hence the name 'Palace of Winds.'

Hawa Mahal is a perfect example of Rajputana and Mughal architecture blending together. The palace is a five-story building with a pyramid shape and is adorned with beautiful carvings, arches, and motifs. The top floors of the palace were meant for the royal ladies, and it is said that they used these windows to watch the daily processions and street activities without being noticed by the public.

Architectural Features

The most striking feature of the Hawa Mahal is its unique facade, which looks like a giant honeycomb. The windows, known as "Jharokhas," are beautifully decorated with intricate latticework and allow a cool breeze to flow through the palace, providing comfort during the scorching summer months. The palace is constructed with pink sandstone, giving it its signature hue, and the architectural design combines traditional Rajputana elements with Mughal influences.

Visitors can also explore the small museum on the first floor, which showcases artifacts and portraits from the history of the palace and Jaipur's royal family.
