Welcome to Kerala

Kerala, located on the southwestern coast of India, is a paradise known for its serene beaches, lush landscapes, vibrant culture, and rich heritage. It is often referred to as "God's Own Country" due to its abundant natural beauty, including the famous backwaters, hill stations, waterfalls, and wildlife sanctuaries. Kerala is also renowned for its traditional arts, unique cuisine, Ayurveda treatments, and colorful festivals.

Top Destinations in Kerala

Kerala offers a variety of destinations for all kinds of travelers. From beaches to hill stations and backwaters, here are some must-visit places:

Alleppey (Alappuzha)

Known as the "Venice of the East," Alleppey is famous for its enchanting backwaters and houseboat cruises. You can spend a day on a traditional Kerala houseboat, floating through picturesque canals surrounded by lush green landscapes. Alleppey is also home to the beautiful Alappuzha Beach and the historic Ambalappuzha Sri Krishna Temple.

Munnar

Munnar, a hill station nestled in the Western Ghats, is famous for its tea plantations, mist-covered hills, and pristine natural beauty. The town offers scenic trekking routes, waterfalls like Attukal and Cheeyapara, and the Eravikulam National Park, home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr.

Kumarakom

Situated on the Vembanad Lake, Kumarakom is known for its serene backwaters, bird sanctuary, and tranquil houseboat experiences. The Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary is home to a wide variety of migratory birds, making it a popular destination for bird watchers. The calm, soothing atmosphere makes it perfect for a relaxing holiday.

Kovalam Beach

Kovalam is a famous beach destination in Kerala, known for its crescent-shaped beaches and clear blue waters. It is ideal for sunbathing, swimming, and water sports like surfing and parasailing. The Lighthouse Beach, Samudra Beach, and Hawa Beach are popular among tourists.

Thekkady

Thekkady is famous for the Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary, where you can take boat safaris through the Periyar Lake, spotting elephants, tigers, and various wildlife. The spice plantations and the surrounding forest area make it a great destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike.

Kerala Details

Kerala, known as "God's Own Country," is one of the most beautiful places in India. From the enchanting backwaters in Alleppey and Kumarakom to the pristine beaches in Varkala and Kovalam, Kerala offers a unique experience for every traveler. Kerala is also home to wildlife sanctuaries such as Thekkady, which is famous for its spice plantations and the Periyar Tiger Reserve.

Kerala’s culture is deeply rooted in its traditions, which include Kathakali dance, Kalaripayattu martial arts, and various forms of traditional music. The state is famous for its Ayurveda practices, where visitors can indulge in natural therapies and healing treatments. Kerala's cuisine is equally famous, with seafood, coconut-based dishes, and the iconic Kerala Sadya feast being some of the must-try experiences.
