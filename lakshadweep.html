Welcome to Lakshadweep

Lakshadweep, an archipelago of 36 islands off the southwestern coast of India, is a tropical paradise known for its white sandy beaches, crystal-clear waters, and abundant marine life. This pristine region offers an idyllic escape for nature lovers, adventure enthusiasts, and those seeking tranquility.

Transportation to Lakshadweep

Travel options to Lakshadweep from LPU:

  • Kochi (Main departure hub for Lakshadweep Islands): ~2,200 km from LPU
    • Flight: ₹5,000 - ₹10,000 (to Cochin International Airport)
    • Train: ₹500 - ₹1,500 (to Ernakulam Railway Station, Kochi)
  • From Kochi to Lakshadweep Islands:
    • Flight: ₹5,000 - ₹8,000 (to Agatti Airport, Lakshadweep)
    • Boat: ₹2,000 - ₹3,000 (approximately 14-20 hours by ferry to Agatti Island)

Top Destinations in Lakshadweep

Agatti Island

Agatti Island is the gateway to Lakshadweep and one of the most popular islands in the archipelago. Famous for its coral reefs, turquoise lagoons, and white-sand beaches, it's perfect for water sports like snorkeling and scuba diving.

Kavaratti Island

Kavaratti is the administrative capital of Lakshadweep. It is known for its clean beaches, lush greenery, and the beautiful Ujra Mosque. The island offers a range of water activities like kayaking, swimming, and diving.

Kalapeni Island

Kalapeni is a peaceful, less-explored island in Lakshadweep. It is famous for its pristine white beaches, coral reefs, and ideal conditions for snorkeling and swimming in crystal-clear waters.

Bangaram Island

Bangaram Island is known for its natural beauty, with calm lagoons and coral reefs. It’s an excellent spot for diving and a perfect place to unwind in a quiet, secluded atmosphere.
